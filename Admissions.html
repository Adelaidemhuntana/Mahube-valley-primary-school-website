School Admission Information

As stipulated in the South African Schools Act of 1996, all children between the ages of 7 and 15 are compelled to attend school.

Attendance Requirements

Parents and guardians should ensure that all learners of this age are registered to go to school and that their children attend school regularly.

Registration for the Following Year

It is the responsibility of every parent and guardian to ensure that their children are registered for the following year, well before the end of the current school year.

Parents may register children who are supposed to start school next year, near their home or workplace, between April and October of each year. It is important to check the registration deadlines of individual provinces.

Admission Policy for Public Schools

According to the Admission Policy for Ordinary Public Schools:

  • Grade R: Age 4, turning 5 by 30 June in the year of admission.
  • Grade 1: Age 5, turning 6 by 30 June in the year of admission.

The principal of the school must give a parent/guardian the admission policy of the school and explain it. A public school may not administer any test for admission to a school.

Documents Required for Application

When making an application, the following documents are needed:

  • Birth certificate (Note: baptismal certificates are not proof of birth date).
  • Immunisation card.
  • Transfer card or last school report card (for learners who have been to school previously).

Additional Documents for Non-South African Citizens

If the child is not a South African citizen, the following documents are required:

  • Study Permit.
  • Temporary or permanent residence permit from the Department of Home Affairs.
  • Evidence of application for permission to stay in South Africa.

In Gauteng, the process for enrolling children in Grade 1 and Grade 8 government schools is conducted online through the Gauteng Department of Education (GDE) Online Admissions System.

Steps for Enrolment:

  1. Visit the GDE Online Admissions Website:
    Website: www.gdeadmissions.gov.za
  2. Register as a Parent/Guardian:
    • Create an account using your ID number, email address, and phone number.
    • Ensure all contact details are accurate, as notifications will be sent to these.
  3. Enter the Learner’s Details:
    • Provide your child’s name, ID number (or birth certificate number), and the grade they are applying for (Grade 1 or Grade 8).
  4. Choose Schools:
    • Select up to five preferred schools for your child.
    • You can prioritize schools based on your home address, work address, or sibling connections.
    • Schools near your home or workplace will be given preference.
  5. Submit Documentation:
    After completing the online application, submit the following documents to the selected schools within seven days:
    • Child’s birth certificate
    • Proof of home address
    • Immunization card (for Grade 1)
    • Latest school report (for Grade 8)
    • Parent/guardian ID
  6. Await Placement:
    • The Department of Education will review your application and place your child in a school based on availability and preferences.
    • Notifications of placement will be sent via SMS and email.
  7. Accept Placement:
    • Once your child is placed, log in to the GDE portal to accept the placement offer within the given time frame.

Important Notes:

  • Ensure you apply during the designated enrolment period to avoid missing deadlines.
  • Admission is subject to space availability in the preferred schools.

For more information, visit the official GDE website: www.education.gauteng.gov.za
